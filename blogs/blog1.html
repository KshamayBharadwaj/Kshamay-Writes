From Fear to Confidence: My Journey in Public Speaking

Kshamay P Bharadwaj February 8, 2025

Public speaking was never my strength. I was always the shy one, struggling to express my thoughts easily and overthinking that I might be wasting others' time. This fear stuck with me throughout my life and especially during my college years, where I was pushed into situations that demanded me to step out of my comfort zone.

The Beginning: Shyness & Self-Doubt

From the start, I had always been nervous about speaking in front of others. My fear of public speaking didn’t develop over time; it had been there since I was young. I avoided speaking in front of a group, partly because I felt I couldn’t express myself clearly and was worried about what others would think.

The First Big Challenge: Pitching My Idea

My first big challenge came during a college ideathon, where I had to pitch an idea alone. I remember being so nervous that I wanted to back out. The thought of speaking in front of others, especially people from different years of college, made me anxious. But peer pressure got to me, and I decided to give it a try.

Preparing for the Pitch

Since I was the only member in the team, I didn’t prepare too much for the actual pitch. I had researched my idea thoroughly, but when it came to presenting, I didn’t know how I would express everything clearly. I had the knowledge, but I couldn’t translate my thoughts into words effectively.

The Pitch: Nervous, But Pushing Through

When I finally stood up to present, my mind was filled with doubt. I knew what I needed to say, but the words didn’t come out smoothly. It felt like my thoughts were stuck, and I couldn’t express them as clearly as I had hoped. Even though I struggled, I kept going and finished the pitch. It was a huge relief when it was over.

Feedback & Motivation

After the pitch, the judges gave me feedback, saying that for a first-year student, I had done well and encouraged me to keep going. They appreciated the fact that I started early in my journey. That feedback meant a lot to me and boosted my confidence significantly. I realized that people don't expect perfection, and their support made me feel like I could improve with time.

Lessons Learned

  • Don’t Overthink: The audience isn’t judging you as much as you think. They’re more focused on the message, not on how perfectly it’s delivered.
  • Preparation is Key: Even though I didn’t prepare my pitch as much as I should have, I realized that better preparation would have made the process smoother.
  • Feedback Helps: Positive feedback can be a huge confidence booster, but constructive criticism helps you improve.
  • Keep Pushing Yourself: Public speaking may always have a little bit of nervousness, but the more you do it, the easier it becomes.

Looking Ahead

Since that event, my confidence has definitely grown. Although the fear of public speaking still affects me at times, I know that with practice and experience, I’ll continue to improve. I’m determined to make the next event better, and I’m not backing down from new challenges.

To Anyone Struggling with Public Speaking

If you’re someone who gets nervous about speaking in front of people, I want to leave you with one piece of advice: Don’t overthink what the audience is thinking. They don’t have that much time to focus on you. Just do your thing, and focus on getting your message across. The more you practice, the more confident you’ll become.
